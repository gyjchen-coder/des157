X-RAY 101

An X-ray shows different shades of grays that represent different tissues and bones. Denser structures like bone and metal absorb and block more X-rays, so they appear white, while less dense materials like air allow X-rays to pass through and appear black, with soft tissues appearing in shades of gray in between.
